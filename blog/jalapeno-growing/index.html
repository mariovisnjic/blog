My two-year project of growing jalapeños

Aug 26, 2024

From writing last time till now, I have completely redone looks of the blog, and it is completely static without any framework.

I like eating jalapeños. They are just right amount of hot for me and are bringing nice taste of green pepper. Every developer has a dream of leaving all technology behind and becoming a farmer. So instead of usual online ordering, I wanted to see if I could grow one and enjoy a reward of hard work.

Growth

Started with 20 seeds of jalapeño and hot portugal. All of it is gonna be indoors since I don't have other option. Pretty sure that I planted them too early in the year, they took a while to come out. Also, it should complete whole growth in a year. For me, it took two. My guess is very cold spring in first year.

From 20 seeds, 11 sprouted nicely, over time I cut them down to 3 largest and strongest to replant into larger pots. Didn't have enough space to keep them all. Survival of the fittest?

Results

I enjoy eating them with salads or adding them to meals that are lacking a bit of kick. However, three plants produce a lot of peppers, so I decided to make two sauces. One based on peppers and other cooked with tomatoes.

I was surprised how easy all of it is.
Just put it in a dirt, give it sun, warmth and water.
When it is bigger, pluck some leaves and branches that are messing with growth.
Peppers are ready to be picked when they get "stretch marks"

And that is it. I spent more of my time moving them around the apartment so they don't stand in a way and writing this article than actually taking care of them. In the end I gave them away. They gave another round of peppers. Good for them.

To conclude. It was easier than expected. Might return to it when I have some patch of land to grow them properly.
